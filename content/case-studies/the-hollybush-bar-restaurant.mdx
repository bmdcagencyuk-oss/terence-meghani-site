## (01) The Challenge

Bar-restaurant brands in any local hospitality market are plural and crowded. Customers choose between five or six options within a short walk, often on impulse and increasingly via a quick scan of social and search results before they leave the house. The Hollybush came in with an established customer base, a specific character built around food, drinks and atmosphere, and a loyal local following — but with a visual identity that did not reflect what made the venue distinctive. The brand was quietly working against the venue rather than supporting it.

The brief was to develop a hospitality brand identity that positioned the venue as a modern, confident destination without sacrificing the warmth and character that already brought customers in. The work had to land at every touchpoint customers encounter — the menu in their hands, the business card in their wallet, the social post in their feed, the signage outside the venue — without each artefact feeling produced in isolation. Coherence across the day-to-day operational materials was as important as the headline mark itself.

## (02) The Approach

Discovery included structured interviews with staff and patrons alongside market analysis of the local dining and bar scene, mapping where competing venues sat on tone, price point and visual presentation. The resulting insight informed the direction of the identity: confident, warm, considered, and unmistakably local — modern enough to read well on a contemporary feed, settled enough to feel like it belonged to a venue with history.

The identity itself pairs an elegant wordmark with carefully crafted business cards and menu layouts, each designed with the same attention to detail the venue brings to service. Menus were treated as brand artefacts as much as operational documents — layout, hierarchy, typography and paper stock all calibrated to support the dining experience rather than compete with it. The system was extended into supporting marketing collateral so that social posts, printed flyers, signage and promotional pieces all carry the same visual logic without needing fresh design work each time.

## (03) The Outcome

A complete bar-restaurant brand identity was delivered across logo, business cards, menu design and supporting marketing collateral. The system gives the venue a consistent visual presence from the moment a customer first encounters the brand on social or in search through to the menu they read at the table — every artefact part of the same coherent identity rather than separately produced pieces.

The brand has continued to serve the business across day-to-day operations and seasonal updates, with the menu and collateral templates extending naturally into food and drink updates, special-event materials and ongoing marketing pushes. The combined work has settled into the operating rhythm of the venue, supporting the warmth and confidence the team brings in person with a brand presence that finally reflects the standard of the experience.
