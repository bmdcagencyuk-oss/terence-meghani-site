## (01) The Challenge

TEDx events operate under tight licensing constraints. The TED brand guidelines are non-negotiable — typography, logo usage, colour relationships — and local organisers are expected to produce a complete event programme without modifying any of those fixed elements. The challenge, every year, is to make a TEDx event feel distinctive while staying completely within-guideline.

The University of Salford's independently-licensed edition needed a creative programme that would uphold the prestige associated with the TED platform, resonate with a university-based audience, and travel across every promotional touchpoint — from Instagram squares to concourse banners — without compromise.

## (02) The Approach

The approach started with a rigorous audit of what the TEDx guidelines permit versus what they prohibit. Within that brief, a local visual system was developed that layered custom typography, imagery, and motion-ready graphics on top of the TEDx core mark. Every promotional asset — social, print, banner, digital display, email — was built from a single design system, so assets scaled from 4K venue screens down to a phone thumbnail without rework.

## (03) The Outcome

A full promotional asset library delivered across digital and print. The campaign ran consistently across pre-event marketing, on-day collateral, and post-event content.
