## (01) The Challenge

Independent garages compete against dealership service departments and national chains on trust, not price. The visual category is notoriously poor — clip-art logos, generic "we service all makes" taglines, stock imagery of disembodied spanners — which means a garage doing genuinely strong technical work often looks indistinguishable from one that is not. Protech Autocentres had strong technical work, an established local following and the kind of repeat customer relationships that take years to build, but a brand identity that was actively understating the operation.

The remit covered the full stack of brand and marketing. Identity, commissioned photography, website and an ongoing digital marketing framework all needed to land at the same level — a unified automotive brand that could sit credibly alongside the dealerships Protech were quietly outperforming, and reassure a first-time customer landing on a postcode search that this was a serious independent specialist rather than a generic local garage.

## (02) The Approach

The identity was rebuilt from the ground up. Discovery and competitor research came first, looking carefully at how dealership service departments, national fast-fit chains and independent specialists present themselves online and in person. The wordmark and supporting visual system were then built to position Protech with the seriousness of a dealership without the corporate distance — colour, typography and tone calibrated to feel authoritative rather than chummy, and engineered to read consistently across signage, vehicle livery, uniforms, digital and printed collateral.

Photography was commissioned on-site at the workshop, featuring real customer vehicles, real technicians and real working bays, replacing category-standard stock imagery entirely. The website was rebuilt around the way customers actually arrive — local search, mobile-first, a direct path from service intent to enquiry — and the digital marketing programme extended the same visual logic into paid search, paid social and organic channels. The system was designed to be operated and refreshed by the in-house team after handover.

## (03) The Outcome

A full automotive brand identity system was delivered alongside a commissioned brand photography library, a new website and a structured ongoing marketing framework covering paid search, paid social, organic content and review capture. The combined work gave Protech a coherent commercial presence — visible in local search, recognisable on the forecourt, consistent in print — that sits alongside dealership presentation without losing the credibility of an independent specialist.

The brand and marketing system has continued to serve the business beyond the launch phase, supporting sustained, high-quality local enquiry volume and giving the team a defensible visual identity to extend across new service lines, additional sites and seasonal campaigns. The photography library remains in active use, and the marketing framework continues to run as the primary inbound channel for new customers across the catchment.
