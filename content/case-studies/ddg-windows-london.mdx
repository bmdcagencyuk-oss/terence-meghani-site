## (01) The Challenge

Home improvement is a category defined by trust-building across long sales cycles. Customers rarely buy windows twice in a hurry — and when they do, it is often a decade or more later, after researching multiple suppliers, reading reviews, and asking neighbours. For DDG Windows, a London-based double-glazing specialist working across the wider capital, the brand needed to convey reliability, professionalism and longevity without looking dated, and without falling into the cluttered, deal-led visual patterns that dominate the wider double-glazing and home improvement category.

The brief was a focused brand refresh rather than a full identity rebuild. DDG had an established trading history, a loyal customer base across London, and an existing book of referral work — what was missing was a visual identity that matched the standard of the installation work and could carry the business forward through the next phase of growth. The refresh had to feel like a quiet upgrade rather than a reinvention, recognisable to existing customers and credible to new ones.

## (02) The Approach

Work began with detailed research into DDG's market position, customer base and the wider London double-glazing landscape, looking carefully at how competing installers — large national chains and smaller independents — present themselves and where genuine differentiation was available. The new mark was built around a sleek, stylised window form, providing a subtle visual cue back to the product without leaning on literal illustration, paired with a modern, clean typeface chosen for legibility across every context the brand needs to land in: van livery, signage, quote documents, business cards and digital.

The palette was anchored on a confident blue with a balancing grey — a considered combination calibrated to convey professionalism, reliability and a sense of longevity. Brand guidelines were produced to document the system, with clear usage rules so that the visual identity holds together when applied across vehicles, printed marketing, customer-facing quote documents and digital touchpoints by different members of the team. The system was deliberately built to be workable for a business operating day-to-day across London rather than precious about every application.

## (03) The Outcome

A complete brand refresh was delivered, applied across primary identity, supporting visual system, brand guidelines and the core collateral that a working London double-glazing business needs in daily use — vehicle livery, signage, business cards, quote and contract documents, and digital touchpoints. The system was handed over with documentation so that future work — additional templates, new collateral, vehicle wraps as the fleet grows — can be produced consistently without further design support.

The refreshed identity has settled into the day-to-day operation of the business, reinforcing the trust signals that matter most in the home improvement category and giving DDG a brand that can be put in front of new customers and existing referrals without explanation. The brand has continued to serve the business across the long sales cycles characteristic of the double-glazing market, supporting sustained credibility across the London catchment the company works in.
