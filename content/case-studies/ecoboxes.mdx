## (01) The Challenge

The sustainable packaging category has become increasingly competitive as more buyers — direct-to-consumer brands, ecommerce operators, food and drink producers — actively shift away from conventional plastics. For Ecoboxes, strong products and a genuine environmental mission were not yet translating into the market reach the business needed. Visibility in organic and paid search was underdeveloped, and brand recognition as a serious player in eco packaging required active building rather than assumption — the buyers in the category are research-led, comparison-driven, and rarely buy on a single touchpoint.

The brief covered a wider digital marketing programme rather than a single-channel push: brand sharpening, a more capable web presence, paid search activity calibrated to the actual buyer journey in B2B sustainable packaging, and content that could pull qualified enquiry traffic from the searches Ecoboxes' customers actually run. The work had to be commercially defensible, not just on-message — the sustainability story matters, but it has to translate into orders.

## (02) The Approach

A coordinated digital marketing programme was built around Ecoboxes' core sustainable packaging positioning. Content was produced addressing the actual decisions buyers make when switching to eco-friendly packaging — material choices, compliance, cost comparisons against conventional packaging, lead times — taking a lead-generation approach rather than awareness-only blogging. The brand voice and visual system were tightened so that the same tone carries across paid ads, organic content, the website and outbound communications without each channel reading as a separate exercise.

Paid search and paid social campaigns were structured around product intent and buyer-stage, rather than broad sustainability awareness keywords that would have spent budget without converting. Landing pages and product content were aligned to the campaigns to give a consistent path from ad click to enquiry. PPC, organic content and email were operated as a single system, with the work designed to be handed back to the internal team with templates, documentation and a content cadence the business could maintain.

## (03) The Outcome

A full digital marketing programme was delivered live across paid and organic channels — paid search, paid social, content, on-site SEO and supporting creative assets — with the brand voice, visual system and content framework handed over for ongoing internal use. Ecoboxes finished the engagement with a coherent commercial digital presence rather than a collection of unconnected tactics, and a documented playbook for keeping it running.

The work has continued to serve the business as the foundation of how Ecoboxes presents itself to the sustainable packaging market. The PPC programme acts as the primary inbound channel for new customer enquiries, the content library continues to support organic visibility for the buying searches that matter, and the brand and visual system remain the consistent layer underneath every customer-facing campaign — supporting sustained, qualified enquiry volume from the buyers the business is built to serve.
